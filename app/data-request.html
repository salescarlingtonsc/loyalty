Your personal data

Make a data request

Use this process to request access, correction, consent withdrawal, deletion or closure for personal data handled through Frenly.

Last updated: 19 July 2026

Start with the business you know If your request concerns a purchase, appointment, reward, membership or marketing message, the merchant normally controls that record. Contacting the merchant first is usually fastest. You may still contact Frenly and we will route or support the request appropriately.

What you can request

Access

Ask what personal data an organisation holds about you and, where applicable, how it was used or disclosed.

Correction

Ask for an error or omission in your personal data to be corrected.

Withdraw consent

Withdraw consent with reasonable notice. We will explain likely service consequences before acting.

Deletion or closure

Ask for data deletion or account closure. Some records may need to remain for legal, security or legitimate business purposes.

How the process works

Tell us what you need

Email the Privacy Contact using the details below. Be specific about the merchant, record and request type so we do not disclose or change the wrong information.

We acknowledge and triage it

We will acknowledge receipt, identify whether Frenly or the merchant is responsible, and explain the next step. We may ask the merchant to participate.

We verify identity proportionately

Before releasing or changing data, we match limited information against the relevant record or use an existing authenticated channel. We will not ask for more identity data than reasonably needed.

We assess and respond

We search relevant systems, consider applicable exceptions and third-party rights, and provide the outcome through a suitable channel. Access requests are handled within applicable PDPA timeframes; if more time is needed, we will notify you as required.

Do not email sensitive identity or payment information.

Do not send your password, one-time code, full payment-card number, bank login, Singpass credentials, NRIC image, passport image or medical records. If stronger verification is genuinely necessary, we will explain a safer and proportionate method.

Representatives, children and urgent security issues

An authorised representative should identify the person represented, describe their authority and provide a way for us to confirm that authority without disclosing unnecessary data. A parent or guardian may make a request concerning a child; we will use age-appropriate and proportionate verification.

For suspected account takeover, exposed booking links, unauthorised access or another urgent security issue, put Urgent security issue in the subject line and describe what happened without including passwords or codes.

Possible outcomes

We may fulfil the request, ask for clarification, coordinate with the merchant, provide access in a protected format, correct a record, stop consent-based processing, close an account, or explain why an exception or continuing legal or business need applies.

A consent withdrawal does not operate retrospectively. It may affect a feature that cannot work without the relevant data. Deletion is not always immediate or complete where records must be preserved for security, disputes, financial obligations, legal requirements or protected backup cycles.

If you are dissatisfied, reply to the Privacy Contact so the concern can be reviewed. You may also seek guidance from or make a complaint to Singapore's Personal Data Protection Commission. See the Privacy Notice for more about roles, purposes, retention and complaints.
